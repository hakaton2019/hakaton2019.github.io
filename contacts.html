Contacts

Note: If you have a question about a specific pet or policies at a shelter, please contact them directly. Asking Shelter will delay your search for a pet, since each shelter manages its own pet list and information on our website. Read on for more answers.

Are you looking for a new pet?

Shelter is a searchable list of pets from thousands of shelters and foster groups. Since our database is updated on a daily basis, we recommend you keep checking back.

Are you a shelter/placement group that needs assistance?

We have specially trained staff dedicated to answering questions from shelter workers and placement groups.

Do you want more information about a pet you’ve seen on Shelter?

Please directly contact the animal welfare organization that is caring for this pet. This information can be found on the group’s home page or under the pet’s picture on their description page. Please don’t contact us about individual pets. We don’t have any information other than what is posted on their page so contacting us will slow your search and time may be of the essence.

Do you need more information on adoption policies?

Adoption policies vary greatly from shelter to shelter, so please don’t contact Shelter for information specific to a local adoption group. It will slow your inquiry! Contact the shelter or placement group directly. If you are interested in a particular pet, please contact the organization that is caring for that animal. You can find their contact information just under the pet’s photo on each pet description page.
Adoption fees range from $5 to hundreds of dollars. Services also range greatly, from virtually none to full vaccinations, spay/neuter, and temperament testing/training. In almost all cases, the adoption fee is less than the actual cost of caring for the pet while it is sheltered. Some groups will not adopt outside their area and many will want to do a “home check” as part of the adoption process.
